# OLC Mission Statement

**Old Line Fidelity Television Distribution**

## Core Values

### 🎬 Timelessness
Preserve the aesthetic and spirit of classic broadcast television. Every frame carries the weight of nostalgia—the hum of the cathode ray, the imperfections that made the medium real.

### 📺 Quality Entertainment
Curate and sequence content that rewards the viewer with unexpected joy. Saturday mornings should feel *earned*, not optimized by an algorithm.

### 🔧 Accessibility
Use only free, open-source tools. FFmpeg. Python. Community. No proprietary black boxes. Anyone should be able to fork, modify, and broadcast.

### 🎨 Aesthetic Fidelity
The VHS filter is not a gimmick—it's a *statement*. Scanlines, color bleed, tracking jitter. These aren't bugs; they're the fingerprints of an era.

### 🌍 Community-Driven
Each broadcast block is a time capsule. Encourage remixing, sampling, and re-broadcasting of content. The archive is the art.

---

## The Vision

**OLC exists to answer a simple question:**

> In an age of infinite, frictionless streaming, can we create television that feels *scheduled*? Deliberate? Handcrafted?

We're not trying to recreate the 1980s. We're trying to capture what made Saturday morning special—the ritual, the surprise, the *human* touch that an algorithm can never replicate.

---

## Pillars of the Project

1. **Preservation**: Archive and restore public domain cartoons, commercials, and forgotten broadcasts.
2. **Automation**: Use intelligent sequencing to generate "new" broadcast blocks from existing content.
3. **Aesthetics**: Apply period-accurate filters and effects to make digital content feel *tactile*.
4. **Community**: Open-source the entire pipeline so others can build their own OLC stations.

---

## How We Work

- **Modular**: Each component (engine, filters, sequencing) can be swapped or extended.
- **Intentional**: Every design choice—from the VHS jitter to the yellow caption font—is deliberate.
- **Transparent**: All code, workflows, and creative decisions are documented and shareable.
- **Respectful**: We honor the original creators and the history of the medium.

---

## The Broadcast Block Philosophy

A single OLC broadcast block (10 minutes) is structured like a real TV station interrupting itself:

1. **Station ID** (15 sec): High-energy intro. Establish mood.
2. **Show Segment A** (4 min): Nostalgia hit. Cartoons, old favorites.
3. **Commercial Break** (1 min): Period ads. Toys, cereal, the dreams of the past.
4. **Show Segment B** (4 min): Variety. Keep the viewer engaged.
5. **Outro** (45 sec): "Coming up next..." A promise that the ritual continues.

Every transition is marked by **VHS static**—a reminder that we're watching *broadcast television*, not Netflix.
